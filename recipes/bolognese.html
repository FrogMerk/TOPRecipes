The Best Bolognese You'll Ever Make

And why nutmeg is slept on

This bolognese recipe literally saved my life, no word of a lie. I was straight up outta beef and had to run to the supermarket. While i was out my house LITERALLY burned down...

Now was that my fault because i left the stovetop on with a teatowel lying all too close by?

Maybe.

Either way, now I have no house, but at least I have this kick-ass bolognese recipe. You should really give this one a go!

Ingredients

  • 1 Brown onion - finely diced
  • 3 Cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 Carrot - finely diced
  • 500g Minced Beef. (Try and get something fatty, lean beef is not what we're after here)
  • 500ml Beef stock, if possible avoid bouillon cubes
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½tsp Nutmeg - please, please buy whole nutmeg and grate it for this, pre-ground just won't be the same
  • A handful of fresh rosemary
  • 400g Tinned tomatoes. (Aim to get something italian here, with real tomato kick)
  • A good glug of red wine

Steps

  1. Firstly we're going to brown off the onions in 2tbsp olive oil over a medium heat. This should make sure they start to break down and get translucent, we want to move on once we start to see some fond developing on the bottom of the pan.

    Once you have that fond developing, add your garlic and give it 30 seconds to cool, or until you can really smell them. Then add your carrots, keep stirring occasionally to make sure nothing catches on the pot.

    Continue to cook your carrots for a further 3-5 minutes depending on how finely they are diced, all the while watching the fond develop, if it's looking like burning, adjust the heat down to compensate.

  2. Once your carrots have sweated enough and your fond is developing, add your beef in and raise the temperature a little. We're looking to brown off the meat and keep adding to that caramelisation on the bottom of the pan.

    Keep everything moving frequently at this point and make sure your stock is on standby.

    Once your meat is mostly browned and the fond is reaching a critical point, pour about 50-80 ml (no need to be too accurate, just eyeball this) into the pot and quickly start to use it to lift the fond off the bottom of the pot, stirring well to incorporate. Turn the heat down at this point.

  3. Add in your herbs now, oregano and rosemary, along with the half teaspoon of nutmeg and season well. This will all add flavour throughout the cook, the nutmeg especially will give this a subtle twist that works incredibly well with the sweetness of the carrots.

  4. Add in the remainder of your stock, the tinned tomatoes and give the bolognese a good glug of red wine to add to the depth of flavour. The pot should now hold quite a lot of liquid, don't worry, this will reduce over time.

    Adjust the heat to bring the pot to a slow simmer and partially cover the pot, to keep heat in, but allow steam to escape, this will reduce the liquid volume and allow it to thicken.

  5. Simmer for at least an hour, up to an hour and a half depending on how thick you want to make it (keep in mind it will thicken further as it cools). If you're cooking pasta alongside, a dash of pasta water will add some starch to help thicken it a little.

    As always, please taste and season as you go, add pepper to taste towards the start of this cooking time, depending on how much bite you want to give it.
